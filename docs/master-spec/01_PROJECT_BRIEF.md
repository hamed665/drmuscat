# DrMuscat Project Brief — V10.1 SEO-First Revision

## 1. Product Definition
DrMuscat is an Oman-first healthcare discovery, SEO, trust, and provider growth platform. It helps public users discover centers, doctors, clinics, pharmacies, labs, imaging, physiotherapy, beauty/skin, dental and wellness providers in Muscat and Oman.

DrMuscat is not a paid user card product. Do not build, sell, price, promote, or require a Health Card or any paid user membership card. Public users are free. Provider revenue comes from center/doctor subscription plans, verified profiles, visibility, offers, analytics, advertising, sponsored placements, media, and future growth tools.

## 2. Core Audience
Public users:
- Oman residents
- Omani users
- English-speaking expats
- Arabic-speaking users
- families searching for healthcare providers
- users comparing locations, services, WhatsApp contact, calls, directions, reviews and offers

Business users:
- healthcare centers
- dental clinics
- skin/laser/beauty clinics
- pharmacies
- labs and imaging centers
- physiotherapy and wellness providers
- individual doctors who want public profiles
- sales/marketing/admin/finance staff

## 3. Launch Languages
Launch public site languages are exactly:
- `en` English, LTR
- `ar` Arabic for Oman, RTL

Arabic content should be clear professional Arabic for SEO pages, with Omani/local phrasing allowed in CTAs, ads, WhatsApp copy, social copy, trust elements and headings where natural.

Not supported in MVP:
- `fa` Persian/Farsi
- `hi` Hindi

Hindi may be considered later only as a limited, human-reviewed public acquisition locale. Persian must not be generated as a public route, sitemap entry, hreflang entry, UI option, SEO page, dictionary or public microsite language in MVP.

## 4. SEO-First Requirement
SEO is not a later add-on. DrMuscat must be implemented SEO-first from the first public release. Public pages must support server-rendered metadata, index/noindex logic, canonical URLs, hreflang for English/Arabic, JSON-LD, sitemap generation, robots rules, breadcrumbs, local intent pages, area/category/service pages, center and doctor profile pages, trust pages, and content quality thresholds.

The uploaded keyword workbook `drmuscat-keywords-full.xlsx` is a planning source for SEO architecture. It contains 792 keywords: 396 English and 396 Arabic, covering money pages, programmatic category×area pages, cost pages, symptoms, drugs, labs/imaging, wellness, devices, insurance, and local intent.

## 5. No Fake Claims
Do not create fake reviews, fake ratings, fake verified badges, fake medical claims, fake partner status, fake offers, fake analytics, fake discounts, fake doctor ranking, or fake hospital/center cooperation wording.

## 6. Provider Value Proposition
A center or doctor should feel DrMuscat is worth paying for because it provides:
- SEO visibility
- premium public profile
- claim/verification
- WhatsApp/call/direction tracking
- offer visibility without user card sales
- reviews and reputation management
- analytics and monthly reporting
- paid placement/ads
- media and content tools
- sales support and CRM

## 7. User Access Principle
Public browsing is free. Users can browse centers, doctors, services, areas, articles and public offers without registration. Registration is free and must be encouraged through soft notification/nudge prompts, not forced before discovery.

Registration may be required only for actions that need identity or anti-abuse control such as review submission, saving favorites across devices, offer claim codes, personalized alerts, appointment reminders, or account history.
