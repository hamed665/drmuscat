## 1. Purpose

This specification defines future legal, trust, medical disclaimer, AI editorial, sponsored content, privacy, consent, and moderation policy planning for DrMuscat. It is a governance planning document only and does not create final legal language, public routes, CMS entries, runtime enforcement, or policy activation.

The policy system must protect:

- users
- patients
- providers
- doctors
- DrMuscat brand trust
- SEO quality
- medical safety
- privacy
- platform compliance
- AI governance
- sponsored content transparency

No final legal text or public pages are implemented.

## 3. Medical Disclaimer Policy Planning

- DrMuscat is a discovery and information platform, not a medical provider.
- Content is not diagnosis.
- Content is not treatment advice.
- Content is not prescription guidance.
- Users must consult qualified healthcare professionals.
- Emergency situations require local emergency services or urgent care.
- Provider listings do not guarantee suitability.
- Offers do not replace medical judgment.
- AI-generated drafts require human review.
- High-risk content requires medical review before public use.

No final disclaimer text is implemented.

## 4. Editorial Policy Planning

Editorial principles:

- people-first content
- Oman-first local relevance
- factual accuracy
- visible useful content
- no thin programmatic pages
- no fake schema
- no fake reviews
- no unsupported “best” claims
- no hidden sponsored influence
- no keyword stuffing
- English/Arabic quality
- Persian internal-only use
- source review for high-risk claims
- last reviewed date where appropriate
- author/reviewer information where appropriate

## 5. AI Content Policy Planning

- AI may assist with briefs/drafts/summaries.
- AI never publishes.
- AI never approves.
- AI never replaces medical/legal/editorial review.
- AI output must be reviewed by humans.
- AI must not invent provider facts.
- AI must not invent citations.
- AI must not create public Persian/Hindi content.
- AI must not generate diagnosis/prescription/treatment/emergency/lab interpretation advice.
- AI use should be auditable.
- AI-generated or AI-assisted content labeling policy must be approved before public implementation.

## 6. Medical Review Policy Planning

- High-risk content requires medical reviewer approval.
- Medium-risk content may require medical review depending on topic.
- Low-risk discovery content may not require medical review.
- Reviewer role and qualifications must be approved before implementation.
- Medical reviewer decisions must be auditable.
- Medical review date should be tracked.
- Reviewed content should include a last reviewed date where appropriate.
- Medical reviewer participation cannot be implied if no review occurred.

High-risk examples:

- symptoms
- diagnosis
- medications
- treatment decisions
- emergency care
- pregnancy/children/high-risk topics
- lab result interpretation
- prescriptions
- invasive procedures
- before/after claims
- weight loss medications/injections
- mental health/medical advice

## 7. Provider Content Policy Planning

- Providers may request/draft content only through a future approved workflow.
- Providers cannot directly publish.
- Provider facts must come from approved/verified sources.
- Providers cannot self-label as verified/sponsored.
- No fake credentials are allowed.
- No fake awards are allowed.
- No fake facilities/equipment are allowed.
- No unsupported outcome claims are allowed.
- No private patient data is allowed.
- No review manipulation is allowed.
- Content may require admin/medical/legal review.

## 8. Sponsored Content and Ads Policy Planning

- Sponsored content must be clearly labeled.
- Paid placement must not be hidden.
- Ads must not imply medical endorsement.
- Sponsored content must pass quality/review gates.
- Offers must show terms, eligibility, and expiry.
- Fake scarcity is not allowed.
- Misleading discount claims are not allowed.
- Guaranteed medical/beauty outcomes are not allowed.
- Hidden organic ranking boosts are not allowed.
- Ad/sponsored schema eligibility requires future approval.

## 9. Review and Rating Policy Planning

- Ratings/reviews are future-only.
- No seeded reviews are allowed.
- No fake reviews are allowed.
- No provider self-reviews are allowed.
- No review gating/manipulation is allowed.
- No AggregateRating schema is allowed until real approved review system maturity exists.
- Review moderation policy is required before launch.
- Provider reply policy is required.
- Dispute/removal policy is required.
- User login/identity rules are required.
- Privacy and defamation rules are required.

## 10. Comment and Moderation Policy Planning

- Comments require login.
- Anonymous comments are not allowed.
- Moderation is required before public display in early phases.
- Private medical data must not be displayed.
- Abusive/spam/defamatory content is rejected.
- Medical diagnosis/prescription/emergency requests are rejected or redirected safely.
- Provider replies are moderated.
- Admin escalation rules are needed.
- Legal/privacy escalation rules are needed.

## 11. Media and Consent Policy Planning

- Private patient media is not allowed.
- People/patient imagery requires a consent policy.
- Before/after media is blocked until strict policy approval.
- AI-generated fake before/after media is not allowed.
- Misleading edits are not allowed.
- Stolen images are not allowed.
- Fake provider/doctor/facility images are not allowed.
- Media must follow accessibility/performance rules.
- Sponsored media must be labeled.
- Media takedown/removal policy is required.

## 12. Privacy and Data Handling Policy Planning

- Private patient data must not appear in public content.
- PHI must not appear in analytics/reporting.
- Sensitive search text must not be stored unless approved.
- Provider confidential data must be scoped by role.
- Analytics/cookie policy is required before tracking.
- AI data handling policy is required before AI integration.
- Deletion/removal requests require a process.
- Access controls must be server-side.
- Audit logs must avoid leaking sensitive data.

## 13. Trust and Verification Policy Planning

- Provider verification status must be transparent.
- Claimed/verified/sponsored distinctions must be clear.
- Verified does not mean medical endorsement.
- Listing approval does not guarantee quality or suitability.
- Provider data correction process is required.
- Report incorrect information flow is required.
- Expired/closed provider handling policy is required.
- Doctor-provider relationship updates are required.
- Fake trust badges are not allowed.

## 14. Legal/Trust Page SEO Rules

- Trust/legal pages should be accessible and linked.
- Index/noindex decision must be made per page.
- Canonical and hreflang are required for public indexable legal/trust pages.
- Thin placeholder legal pages are not allowed.
- Arabic legal/trust content must be real localized content.
- Public Persian/Hindi policy routes are not allowed unless future approved.
- Medical disclaimer should be easy to find.
- Editorial and AI policy pages should support trust and transparency.
